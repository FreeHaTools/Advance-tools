How Away Simulator works

History replay

Every day the simulator looks at what your selected lights and switches really did N days ago (default 7 — the same weekday last week), and repeats those on/off actions today. Each action is shifted by a random offset (the "randomness" setting) so no two days are identical.

Why it beats random lights

  • Random timers look random — lights at 3 AM, kitchen dark at dinner time. Anyone watching for a few evenings spots the pattern.
  • Replaying your own history reproduces your rhythm: the hallway light on your usual wake-up time, the living room lit on movie night, the porch light off when you normally sleep.
  • The jitter keeps it from being a perfect copy of last week.

Requirements

  • Recorder history must be enabled for the chosen entities and reach back at least as far as your "days back" setting (HA keeps 10 days by default).
  • The Advance Tools add-on must keep running while you're away — the schedule is executed by this add-on, not by HA automations.
  • If an entity has no on/off history on the reference day it is listed as skipped for that day.

Tips

  • Enable it before you leave — with "pause when someone is home" on, it stays silent until everyone is gone.
  • Use the ⚡ blink test on each entity once, so you know the add-on can really control it.
  • Pick a normal week as reference: if last week was a holiday, set days back to 14.
