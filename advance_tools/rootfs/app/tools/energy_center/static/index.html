How Energy Center works

Long-term statistics

Home Assistant's recorder keeps long-term statistics for every energy sensor: one compact summary per hour that is never purged, even when normal history is deleted after a few days. This tool reads that data — the exact same source HA's built-in Energy dashboard uses — so your monthly totals stay accurate forever. Note: hourly detail older than your recorder's retention still exists, but data only starts accumulating from the moment a sensor first reports.

kW vs kWh — power vs energy

  • W / kW (power) is how hard a device is working right now — like the speedometer of a car.
  • Wh / kWh (energy) is how much it consumed over time — like the odometer. Your utility bills you per kWh.

Energy Center only tracks energy sensors (kWh, Wh, MWh) whose value keeps increasing like a meter.

Getting per-device energy

  • Smart plugs with energy monitoring (Shelly, TP-Link Kasa/Tapo, Sonoff POW, Zigbee plugs…) usually expose a kWh sensor out of the box — just track it here.
  • Only have a W (power) sensor? Create a Riemann sum integral helper: open the Helper Maker tool → New Helper → Integral, pick your power sensor, method left, unit prefix k, unit time hours. That gives you a kWh sensor you can track here about an hour later.

How cost is computed

Cost = consumption × your flat price per kWh:

cost = kWh_in_range × price_per_kWh
e.g.  12.4 kWh × 0.24 = 2.98

Find your price on your electricity bill (the per-kWh rate). If you have a variable/tiered tariff, use your average rate — the totals will be a close estimate rather than exact.

Energy Summary dashboard widget

Dashboards (wall tablets) can show a read-only Energy Summary widget with the total consumption, cost and top consumers for a range. Any user who is logged in and has access to that dashboard can see it — no admin rights needed, and nothing can be changed from the widget. Summaries are cached for two minutes, so polling tablets never strain the recorder. You can switch the widget off with "Allow the Energy Summary widget on dashboards" in ⚙ Setup.

Good to know

  • Statistics are written once an hour — a brand-new sensor shows data after roughly an hour.
  • "Today" and "Yesterday" show hourly buckets; "This week" and "This month" show daily buckets.
  • Meter resets (a sensor going back to 0) are handled by HA's statistics engine, so totals stay correct.
  • The dashboard auto-refreshes every 60 seconds.
